## Access Statistics {#access_statistics}

In the **Access Statistics** tab, you can check when each SQL performs a Full Scan, its type, and how many times it occurs. Queries that frequently trigger Full Scans can be optimized to use indexes, improving performance and reducing resource usage.

1. Go to the **Access Statistics** tab in <Cmdname sid="side_analysis" className="uitext" /> > <Cmdname sid="side_dbSqlPlanAnalysis" className="uitext" />.

1. Select the query time and instance.

1. Set the <Cmdname sid="time" className="uitext" /> and <Cmdname sid="instance" className="uitext" /> options, then click the ![Search icon](/img/ico-btn-search.png) button.  
   If needed, you can also configure the <Cmdname sid="filter" className="uitext" /> option to filter the results.

1. Review the search results in the **Access Count** and **Operation Count** sections.

    * **Access Count**: A bar chart showing the number of Full Scans by time range.  
      Selecting a specific time displays the list of SQL statements executed during that time in the bottom list and updates the **Operation Count** chart on the right.

    * **Operation Count**: Displays the types and counts of Full Scans.  
      Hovering over each bar shows the object name.

        ![Operation Count](/img/db-analysis-sql-access-statistics-op-count.png)

### Filtering the searched results

You can filter the retrieved results based on the following criteria:

* **Access Type**: Filter results by **Full Scan** or **Index Scan**.

* **Object Name**: Filter results by the object name.

#### Adding the access type filtering conditions

1. In the <Cmdname sid="filter" className="uitext" /> option at the top of the screen, click the <LinkImage img="ico-add.svg" className="ico" /> button.

1. In <Cmdname sid="TTL06737" className="uitext" />, select the **Access Type** item and the <Cmdname sid="equal" className="uitext" /> condition, then choose either **FULL** or **INDEX**.

1. Click the <Cmdname sid="apply" className="uitext" /> button.

1. To apply the selected conditions to the SQL list, click the ![Search icon](/img/ico-btn-search.png) button.

:::note

To search all for Full Scan and Index Scan, in <Cmdname sid="TTL06737" className="uitext" />, select all options for **FULL** and **INDEX**. By selecting the ![Plus icon](/img/ic-s-plus.svg) button, you add more filtering options.

:::

#### Adding the object name filtering conditions

1. In the <Cmdname sid="filter" className="uitext" /> option at the top of the screen, click the <LinkImage img="ico-add.svg" className="ico" /> button.

1. In <Cmdname sid="TTL06617" className="uitext" />, select the **Object Name** item and the <Cmdname sid="equal" className="uitext" /> condition.

1. Click the empty input field and choose the desired object name from the displayed object list.

1. Click the <Cmdname sid="apply" className="uitext" /> button.

1. To apply the selected conditions to the SQL list, click the ![Search icon](/img/ico-btn-search.png) button.

:::note

**Adding and Deleting Filter Conditions**

- To add a filtering condition, click the <Cmdname sid="bsm_button_add" className="uitext" /> button and repeat steps 1~5. Added conditions are applied using the AND (`&&`) operator.

- To delete specific conditions while adding, click the ![Delete icon](/img/ico-trash.svg) button on the right side of the filter condition. To delete all conditions, click the ![Delete icon](/img/ico-trash.svg) <Cmdname sid="BTN06616" className="uitext" /> button.

- To quickly remove all applied conditions from the <Cmdname sid="filter" className="uitext" /> option, click the <LinkImage img="ico-close.svg" className="ico" /> button.

:::

#### Modifying the filter conditions

1. Click the applied item in the <Cmdname sid="filter" className="uitext" /> option at the top of the screen.

1. In the <Cmdname sid="TTL06737" className="uitext" /> window, edit the desired item and click the <Cmdname sid="apply" className="uitext" /> button.

## Plan Change History {#plan_change_history}

In the **Plan Change History** tab, even if the SQL ID is the same, performance can be affected when the execution plan changes.  
By detecting and monitoring plan changes made by the optimizer, you can prevent unnecessary changes and maintain consistent SQL performance.

1. Go to the **Plan Change Historys** tab in <Cmdname sid="side_analysis" className="uitext" /> > <Cmdname sid="side_dbSqlPlanAnalysis" className="uitext" />.

1. Select the query time and instance.

1. Set the <Cmdname sid="time" className="uitext" /> and <Cmdname sid="instance" className="uitext" /> options, then click the ![Search icon](/img/ico-btn-search.png) button.

1. In the **Plan Change Count** section, select a specific time period to view the list of plan changes that occurred during that time.

   - **Plan Change Count** section: A bar chart showing the number of plan changes that occurred by time period.

### Checking the plan change details

1. In the **Plan Change Historys** tab of <Cmdname sid="side_dbSqlPlanAnalysis" className="uitext" />, click a specific change item from the list.

1. In the **Query** section, details before and after the plan change are displayed.  
   Compare the differences to identify the cause of performance changes.

    * In the **Query** section, click the ![Open in new window icon](/img/ic-popup.svg) button in the upper-right corner to view the section in a new window.

1. To close the **Query** section, click the ![Close icon](/img/ico-close.svg) button in the upper-right corner.
